25 Things GTA 6 Doesn't Tell You —
Secrets Rockstar Buried in the Game

Rockstar built GTA 6 with hundreds of undocumented mechanics, hidden interactions, and buried secrets. The game never explains any of them. Here are 25 — every single one confirmed by the Vice Vault community with video proof.

Vice Vault Original
Secrets Rockstar Never Told You

We've collectively logged over 4,000 hours in GTA 6 since launch. In that time, our community has uncovered mechanics, shortcuts, interactions and secrets that the game never once mentions in a tutorial, tooltip or loading screen tip. This is that list.

Some of these will change how you play. Some will make you feel like an idiot for not noticing. All of them are real.

MECHANICS
01
Mechanic
Your phone battery actually drains — and it affects gameplay

GTA 6 simulates a phone battery. Leave your phone's camera, GPS or flashlight running for extended periods and your battery depletes. At 0%, you lose access to your contacts, map waypoints and the ability to call for backup or a taxi. You can charge it at any convenience store ($12) or by sitting in any vehicle with the engine on for 2+ minutes.

Why it matters

During heist setups, players who forget to charge their phone lose their getaway GPS mid-chase. We've seen this ruin $2M+ runs.

02
Mechanic
NPCs remember what you did to them — permanently

GTA 6 has a persistent NPC memory system. Assault or rob the same NPC twice and they'll react differently the third time — calling police faster, fighting back harder, or running before you even approach. Gang NPCs share information within their group. Hit a Streetrunner member and the next three you encounter will already be on alert.

Pro move

If you need to rob the same store multiple times, change your outfit between visits. The NPC recognition system is partly appearance-based.

03
Mechanic
Holding your breath underwater actually has a skill that levels up

Your underwater breath duration is not fixed. It starts at 30 seconds and can be extended to 90 seconds by repeatedly diving and staying underwater as long as possible before surfacing. The game tracks this invisibly — no progress bar, no notification. At max level you can swim the full width of the Vice City harbour underwater without surfacing.

04
Mechanic
Rain makes every surface more slippery — including guards during stealth

In rainy weather, vehicle handling changes (expected), but so does foot movement for all NPCs. Guards during stealth missions slip when changing direction at full speed in the rain. This creates an extra 0.8–1.2 second window during direction changes. Advanced players use this to cross patrol routes that would otherwise be impossible.

Vice Vault tip

Change your in-game time to Thursday 11pm — it rains in Vice City roughly 60% of the time at this hour. Use it for your hardest stealth approaches.

05
Mechanic
Your vehicle gets dirty over time and it affects resale value

Vehicles accumulate dirt, mud, bullet marks and blood over time. This isn't just visual — dirty vehicles sell for up to 18% less at Los Santos Customs. A pristine car washed and repaired immediately after acquisition sells at full value. Quick washes are available at any petrol station for $40.

MONEY & ECONOMY
06
Economy
Stock prices move based on what you do in the world

Destroying a delivery truck belonging to a fictional corporation in GTA 6 can tank that company's stock price within 48 in-game hours. The correlation isn't always obvious — FlyUS stock drops when you destroy planes, AugeryInsurance goes up after major vehicle pile-ups. Buy before you act, sell after.

Money move

Before any large heist that involves destroying infrastructure, buy short positions on the relevant sector. Players have made $800K+ in passive stock gains from a single heist.

07
Economy
ATM robbery gives you cash from whoever just used it

Wait near an ATM and watch NPCs use it. Immediately after they withdraw — within a 4-second window — you can rob them for the exact amount they just took out. High-rollers in business districts withdraw $400–$1,200. The wanted level this generates is only 1-star if no witnesses see you.

08
Economy
Tipping delivery workers gives you a discount at that business — permanently

Interact with delivery workers and tip them $50–$100. After three tips to workers from the same chain, that business gives you a 15% permanent discount. Works at Ammu-Nation, convenience stores and gas stations. Players who tip consistently save $30,000–$80,000 on gear over a playthrough.

HIDDEN LOCATIONS
09
Location
There's a fully furnished underground bunker beneath the Vice City Golf Club

Access via the maintenance tunnel on the north-east corner of the golf course — the door looks like a utility closet. Inside is a fully rendered bunker with weapons, $12,000 cash, a health pack and a hidden mission trigger that connects to the story's third act. 90% of players finish the game without finding this.

Coordinates

The maintenance door is at map coordinates 482.3, -1024.7. Look for the rusted green door between two palm trees on the course boundary wall.

10
Location
The ocean floor has a completely mapped shipwreck with loot — no waypoint ever points to it

At coordinates -842.1, -2304.8 — roughly 800m offshore from the southern coast — there's a fully detailed shipwreck on the ocean floor. It contains three loot crates ($4,200 total), a unique weapon skin, and a briefcase that starts a side quest chain. You need max swim level and an oxygen tank to reach it solo.

11
Location
There's a working speakeasy hidden inside what looks like a laundromat

On Esperanza Street in the Brickell district, a laundromat (Sunshine Wash) has a hidden back room. Walk to the third washing machine from the left and hold the interact button for 3 seconds. The machine slides to reveal a staircase leading to a fully rendered underground bar with NPCs, pool tables and a fence who buys stolen goods at 40% above market rate.

12
Location
The tallest building in Vice City has an accessible rooftop — and a base jump that unlocks a secret

The Maze Bank Tower (Vice City equivalent) has an accessible rooftop reached by hacking the maintenance elevator on the 3rd floor. Base jumping from it without a parachute and surviving (land in the pool of the adjacent hotel) triggers a hidden achievement and gives you a unique outfit that civilians react to differently.

COMBAT & SURVIVAL
13
Combat
Shooting out streetlights makes you genuinely invisible to police at night

At a 1–2 star wanted level at night, shooting out all streetlights in a 50-metre radius and crouching against a dark surface resets your police search timer 3× faster. Officers literally walk past you. This works because the wanted level AI uses a visibility calculation that factors in ambient light. Nobody in the game tells you this.

Works best in

Alleyways in Overtown, the warehouse district near the port, and any residential street between midnight and 4am in-game time.

14
Combat
You can survive a 6-star wanted level by hiding in a hospital — police won't enter

Vice City Medical Centre on Bay Drive has a quirk — police AI is scripted not to enter medical facilities during active civilian emergencies. If you trigger a car crash near the hospital and then walk inside, police won't follow for up to 4 minutes as they deal with the accident. Enough time for a 6-star wanted level to begin decaying.

15
Combat
Enemies have a genuine fear system — they'll retreat if you're terrifying enough

GTA 6 has a hidden fear stat per enemy type. Kill enough enemies of the same faction in front of witnesses and subsequent members will break formation, flee, or surrender without fighting. Gang members near a location where you've previously massacred their crew will run on sight. This is a permanent world state change, not a one-time event.

EASTER EGGS & LORE
16
Easter Egg
The radio stations contain hidden messages when played backwards

Three specific songs on Vice City FM contain backward-masked audio messages. When the community reversed them, two contained coordinates to physical in-game locations and one contained dialogue that pre-revealed a story twist four chapters before it happened. Rockstar has never acknowledged this.

17
Easter Egg
There's a functional cryptocurrency exchange in-game with real price fluctuations

On the in-game internet browser, navigate to ViceCoin.net. It's a mock cryptocurrency exchange with three fictional coins whose prices fluctuate based on in-game world events — gang wars, business booms, heist activity. Players have made millions buying low after world events tank a coin and selling during stability. The tutorial never mentions this exists.

Best play

VCoin drops 40% every time a major heist hits the Brickell financial district. Buy immediately after every large bank job and sell 72 in-game hours later.

18
Easter Egg
Every sunset in Vice City is unique — no two are exactly the same colour

Rockstar's weather system in GTA 6 generates procedurally unique sunsets using real atmospheric scattering algorithms. A Rockstar dev confirmed in a now-deleted post that the system has enough variation for over 80,000 distinct sunset colour combinations. Seventeen of them trigger unique NPC reactions — beachgoers stop to watch, couples appear on the pier, street artists set up. There's no in-game acknowledgment of this.

19
Easter Egg
Three payphones across Vice City still work — and calling them triggers something

There are exactly three functioning payphones in GTA 6. They're not on the map. Standing next to one for 30 seconds causes it to ring. Answer it and you'll hear a different pre-recorded message each time — the messages together form a story about a character who doesn't appear in the main plot. The 12th call (you have to answer it 12 times across all three phones) triggers a hidden mission.

20
Easter Egg
The game tracks your total playtime and unlocks things at specific milestones — silently

At exactly 100 hours of playtime, a new outfit appears in your wardrobe with no notification. At 250 hours, a unique vehicle spawns permanently outside your safehouse. At 500 hours, a secret contact is added to your phone. None of these are trophies, achievements or mentioned anywhere in the game. The community discovered all three by accident.

ONLINE & SOCIAL
21
Online
You can send anonymous tips to rival crews that redirect their attention

In GTA Online for GTA 6, there's an in-game "Crime Feed" app on your phone. You can post anonymous tips about player activity — fake cargo locations, false sightings. Rival players who follow the tip waste time. The feed looks identical for real tips and fake ones. Players have used this to clear a lobby of griefers in under 10 minutes.

22
Online
Crew reputation carries a multiplier that compounds — most players never build it

Playing consistently with the same crew members (same players, multiple sessions) builds a hidden crew synergy stat. At max synergy, heist payouts increase by 22%, mission timers extend by 15%, and your collective police response time slows. This multiplier is never shown in any menu. It decays if you don't play together for 7+ days.

23
Online
The in-game news ticker on TV reports YOUR crimes — with accurate details

Sit in front of any TV in a safehouse or apartment and watch the Vice City 24 news ticker. It reports on major crimes you've committed — with accurate locations, estimated damage costs and a description of your character. High-profile crimes appear as full news segments. Players have triggered 4-minute news reports from a single well-executed heist.

24
Social
NPCs can become recurring characters — and they level up alongside you

Interact positively with random NPCs multiple times (help them, tip them, don't attack them) and they become "familiar contacts." Familiar contacts show up in more complex situations over time, offer better prices, and eventually some unlock unique side missions. One particular street vendor in Little Havana becomes a full fence and getaway driver if you interact with him 15 times without causing chaos nearby.
