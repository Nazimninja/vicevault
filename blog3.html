12 Mistakes Every GTA 6 Player Makes
in Their First 10 Hours (And How to Fix Them)

We've watched thousands of players go through the same painful early-game loop. The same 12 errors, in almost the same order, every single time. Here's the full list — and exactly what to do instead.

📅 June 26, 2025 ⏱ 10 min read 🚨 12 mistakes 🎮 All platforms
12
Beginner Guide
Stop Making These Errors

GTA 6 is enormous. The map alone takes 20 minutes to drive across at top speed. The systems are deep, interconnected, and almost entirely unexplained. Rockstar drops you in and expects you to figure it out.

Most players don't. They spend their first 10 hours making the same mistakes, falling behind, and wondering why they feel stuck while their friends seem to be racing ahead. We've documented 12 of the most common — ranked by how much they actually cost you.

How to use this guide

Each mistake includes the fix. Read the mistake, read the fix, apply it immediately. You don't need to finish GTA 6 to course-correct — the early game is forgiving if you know what you're doing.

01
Spending money on a luxury apartment in Week 1
Money

The Vice City Penthouse costs $480,000. It looks incredible. It has a garage, a view, and makes you feel like you've arrived. It also kills your early-game momentum completely.

In Week 1, that $480,000 is the difference between owning two passive income businesses (generating $11,700/day) and having nothing but a fancy loading screen. Players who buy the apartment first typically take 3–4 weeks to reach $1M. Players who skip it get there in 7 days.

💸 Average cost of this mistake: 3 extra weeks of grinding
The fix

Buy the cheapest functional safehouse ($45,000 in Davis). It saves your progress and gives you a respawn point. That's all you need in Week 1. Save the penthouse for when you have $2M+ and it genuinely doesn't affect your income flow.

02
Ignoring the in-game phone entirely
Strategy

Most new players treat the phone as a contact list and nothing else. They miss the Crime Feed app, the ViceCoin exchange, the stock market shortcut, the anonymous tip system, and six other apps that are live from Day 1. The game never highlights any of them.

Players who discover ViceCoin on Day 3 vs Day 21 earn an average of $240,000 more in their first month from compound returns alone.

The fix

Spend 10 minutes on Day 1 opening every app on your phone. Specifically check: the internet browser (navigate to ViceCoin.net and the stock market), the Crime Feed, and the Contacts list where hidden missions get added automatically as you progress.

03
Doing random jobs instead of contact missions
Money

Random jobs pay $4,000–$9,000. Contact missions pay $18,000–$35,000. They take roughly the same amount of time. Players who grind random jobs in the first week earn approximately 60% less per hour than players running contact missions from the same characters.

The switch is simple — but only if you know contact missions exist and how to unlock them.

💸 Average cost: $180,000 in Week 1 earnings left on the table
The fix

After Chapter 2 of the story, you unlock Simeon, then Martin Madrazo, then Gerald as contact mission sources. Prioritise these over all random jobs immediately. Martin Madrazo missions are the highest-paying in the early game — run 4 per session minimum.

04
Going into a heist completely unprepared
Combat

The game unlocks heists at Level 12 and points you at them. New players jump straight in without completing prep missions, without the right crew, without the right gear, and usually on Hard difficulty because the payout looks better. They fail 2–3 times and earn nothing. Sometimes they're griefed by random crew members and lose their take entirely.

The fix

Always complete all 3 prep missions before starting a heist. Always play on Normal for your first run — the pay difference doesn't justify the failure risk. Always use a trusted crew (friends or verified players from the Vice Vault Discord). The first successful heist run is worth 3 failed attempts combined.

05
Skipping story mode to go straight to GTA Online
Strategy

Story mode is not optional if you want to succeed online. Completing story chapters unlocks contact missions, properties, weapons, and heist access in GTA Online. Players who skip story and jump straight online are locked out of the most profitable activities for weeks while they unlock things the slow way.

Story completion also gives you a $500,000 cash bonus upon entering GTA Online for the first time — which most players who skip story never receive.

💸 Average cost: $500,000 bonus missed + 2 weeks of locked content
The fix

Complete at least Chapters 1–4 of story mode before going online. It takes roughly 6–8 hours and unlocks everything you need. The $500K bonus alone covers your first passive income property.

06
Buying the wrong first weapon
Gear

New players walk into Ammu-Nation and buy the Heavy Sniper or the RPG because they look impressive. Both are useless for 90% of early-game scenarios. The RPG destroys your wanted level management and the sniper is overkill for standard missions.

The correct early-game loadout costs $8,400 total. Most players spend $40,000+ on weapons they don't use for 15 hours.

The fix

Buy in this order: Suppressed Pistol ($3,500) → Combat MG ($4,900). That's it for Week 1. The suppressed pistol handles stealth scenarios and keeps your wanted level low. The Combat MG handles everything else. Add a Shotgun only once you're doing loud heists. Resist everything else until you're earning $200K+ per session.

07
Fighting the police instead of escaping them
Combat

This is the most common mistake we see in first-hour gameplay. Player gets a 2-star wanted level, stops their vehicle, gets out, and starts shooting cops. This always escalates to 4–5 stars, results in death, hospital fees ($5,000), and lost weapons. It's also just bad tactics.

The GTA 6 police AI is beatable through movement, not firepower. You cannot outgun a 4-star wanted level. You can absolutely outrun it.

The fix

At 1–2 stars: drive at full speed to the nearest Pay & Spray ($500). At 3 stars: use the darkness exploit — shoot out all streetlights in your immediate area, crouch in an alley, and wait 90 seconds. At 4–5 stars: get off the road entirely. The police AI loses you 3x faster if you're not on a named road. Industrial areas and back alleys are your friends.

08
Not saving the game manually — ever
Strategy

GTA 6 autosaves after missions and major events. It does not autosave during free roam. Players who spend 45 minutes robbing ATMs, collecting side mission rewards, and accumulating cash can lose all of it to a disconnect, a crash, or a gang ambush — because they never manually saved.

We've seen players lose $80,000 in a single free-roam session this way. It's entirely preventable.

The fix

Save manually every 20–30 minutes during free roam. Go to your safehouse and sleep — it saves, advances time, and partially restores health. In GTA Online, deposit cash into your Maze Bank account immediately after earning it. Cash in hand is cash at risk.

09
Playing GTA Online in public lobbies while moving cargo
Online

Public lobbies in GTA Online for GTA 6 contain griefers running Oppressor Mk3s and armoured vehicles who specifically target cargo deliveries. A single cargo run worth $38,000 can be destroyed by another player in under 30 seconds. New players lose entire day's worth of earnings this way regularly.

💸 Average cost: full cargo value + time wasted on resupply
The fix

Do all business resupplies and cargo deliveries in a solo public session. To create one: start an invite-only session, then switch to public via the menu — you'll often get dropped into a near-empty lobby. Alternatively, use the Terrorbyte's countermeasures when moving high-value cargo in busy lobbies.

10
Never checking the in-game TV or radio for intel
Strategy

The Vice City 24 news channel reports on your own crimes with accurate details and dollar amounts. It also broadcasts fictional financial news that correlates with stock movements. Players who watch 5 minutes of TV per session have a consistent informational edge on the stock market.

The radio stations also contain coded information about upcoming events — three specific songs contain backward audio that reveals easter eggs and coordinates. 95% of players never interact with either system.

The fix

Sit in front of your safehouse TV for 3–5 minutes between sessions. Watch the financial ticker on Vice City 24. If a fictional company reports "record losses," short their stock. If they report "expansion plans," buy. The correlation isn't perfect, but it's consistent enough to generate $50K–$150K per week in passive stock gains.

11
Ignoring NPC interactions and the reputation system
Social

GTA 6 has a persistent NPC memory and relationship system that the game never explains. Repeatedly robbing the same NPCs makes them more aggressive and faster to call police. Tipping and helping certain NPCs unlocks them as contacts — some become fences, some become getaway drivers, one becomes a full mission giver.

Players who ignore this system miss 8–12 side missions, one unique vehicle, and access to a fence who pays 40% above market rate for stolen goods.

The fix

Diversify your robbery targets — don't hit the same NPC or business more than once per in-game week. Interact positively with street vendors in Little Havana, Brickell and the Port district. The street vendor on Esperanza Street specifically becomes a full contact after 15 positive interactions — he's worth cultivating from Day 1.

12
Not having a plan before starting each session
Strategy

This is the meta-mistake that enables all the others. Players open GTA 6, load into the world, and then figure out what to do. This costs 15–25 minutes of prime session time per play, adds up to hours per week, and is the primary reason casual players feel like they're not progressing.

The best GTA 6 players treat each session like a work shift — they know what they're doing before they boot the game.

The fix

Before each session, answer three questions: (1) What missions am I running today? (2) What business needs resupplying? (3) What's the one thing I'm saving toward? 60 seconds of planning before you load saves 20 minutes of aimless driving every single session.
